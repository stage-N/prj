FAQ

Q. Where is my data stored?

Everything is stored on your device (SQLite) only. See the Privacy Policy.

Q. Where does WBGT data come from?

We use the Open-Meteo API and Ministry of the Environment heat index data, prioritizing the ministry data for display.

Q. I am not receiving notifications

Check that notifications are allowed under your device's Settings → Notifications → Heat Stroke Alert.

Q. Are ads displayed?

App Open ads are shown when launching and when returning from the background. Ads are not shown during heat stroke warnings. You can limit personalization in your device's ad settings.

Q. What is Rainy Season Mode?

A feature that notifies you about indoor humidity and mold risk in June and July. Check it from the banner on the home screen.

Q. Can I share activity logs?

From the record detail screen, you can share activity logs with photos.

Q. Can I migrate data when changing devices?

Data export/import is not currently supported. Reconfiguration is required when changing devices.

Q. What are activity recommendations?

For walking, running, sports, and other selected activities, we show WBGT limits and messages tailored to the activity. The 24-hour forecast graph also shows activity-specific limit lines.

Q. What is a personal profile?

Setting age group (child, general, elderly) and medical history (optional) adjusts warning thresholds. Settings are optional and can be skipped. This is not medical advice; it adjusts Ministry of the Environment standards for individual differences as reference information.

Q. I want to use it for work or job sites

For work records, CSV export, and site management, use Heat Stroke Recorder Pro (¥3,000/month). A guidance banner may appear in Settings or when you have more than 10 records (up to once every 7 days).
