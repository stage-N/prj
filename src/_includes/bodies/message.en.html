Our journey since 2022

In 2022, I founded STAGEN as a company that solves nearby problems with technology.

The first stage was building the foundation — an end-to-end flow from planning through operations, with low-maintenance, high-reliability development. Next came practical tools rooted in daily work: tax, occupational safety, and invoicing. Un glamorous but unavoidable — small, concrete products placed where they are needed.

Later we expanded into weather, education, and fields not on the original map. Rather than staying in one domain, we place tools where problems appear. Today, nearly 20 products run in parallel.

We are now in the stage of a sustainable one-person model running multiple products. We prioritize durability over scale. Success, for us, means each product quietly taking root in users' daily lives.

Why our products exist

Every STAGEN product starts from my own frustrations or voices close to me.

In tax, running a one-person corporation adds up to roughly ¥560,000 per year in fixed costs — advisory fees, accounting, filings, and notifications. I believe you pay tax professionals for judgment and exceptions, not for a deadline calendar you can manage yourself. That insight led to ZeiCal, which organizes filing deadlines from your fiscal year-end month alone. ZeiCal is not tax advice or filing agency — it is a deadline organizer.

Invoicing was another monthly burden. After invoice reform, full-featured accounting software felt excessive for us. A few invoices per month call for simplicity. Rakubill answers that at a ¥500 one-time purchase.

Residence and immigration procedures are especially personal. Half a day at a counter, documents in Japanese only, opaque fees before you even get a quote from a scrivener — I wanted to remove as much of that opacity as possible. With ZaiTap, procedures you can do yourself are 100% free. For complex cases, you can see cost estimates before consulting a scrivener. It is the most authentic product for STAGEN as a company led by a foreign representative.

For outdoor occupational safety, we offer Heat Stroke Alert (free for the public) and Heat Stroke Recorder Pro (field records, CSV/PDF export). Even a one-person company faces real recording obligations on site. Records are the duty; tools are the means — we aim to deliver what works in the field, not lectures.

In weather, Shower Guard tells you only whether it will rain in five minutes, and Cool Walk helps you decide whether to go out using WBGT, UV, and air quality. In an age of information overload, we reduce the steps needed to decide.

In education, Fuzen teaches functional thinking through play, and Forest School offers learning for ages 3–5 with Kumorin. Forest School came from not wanting to hand my own child apps with ads and data collection — ¥350 one-time, no ads, no extra charges, so parents can share it with confidence.

In media, Touten compares stances across Japan's five major newspapers; Banjem and NewsPrism do the same for Korea and the US. Know the bias, then read with your own judgment — we deliver access to diverse viewpoints as a tool.

StockPulse (three-layer Japanese stock analysis) and Phrase Flow (quotes with historical context) extend the same philosophy: deliver what you need, without excess.

Stages spun off from Phrase Flow are growing too. Today's Scripture (Christianity), Today's Sutra (Buddhism), Today's Quran (Islam), and the cross-faith Today's Words of Religion deliver one scripture a day with historical context and contrasting perspectives. The same stance we value in Touten and NewsPrism — never asserting a single position, but placing multiple viewpoints side by side so you can decide for yourself — we brought into the delicate domain of religion. The goal is not to promote any faith, but to offer access to diverse wisdom as a tool.

Going further, Stillpoint turns Phrase Flow's quote library into "kindling for meditation." Sit with today's thought, reflect aloud, and the AI returns deeper, Socratic questions. A library of words that began as a tool for reading grows into a tool for thinking — never isolating one product, but bridging accumulated assets to another stage. That is how STAGEN walks. Stillpoint does not give answers; it quietly supports daily reflection and is not a substitute for medical or psychological care.

For reading, there is PagePace. Register books by ISBN, measure your reading pace (minutes per page) in focused sessions, and visualize a lasting rhythm through streaks and a calendar. Just as Heat Stroke Recorder visualizes on-site records and ZeiCal visualizes deadlines, PagePace visualizes the habit of reading. Rather than the result — books finished or percent complete — it turns your attention to your own rhythm, applying STAGEN's shared philosophy of "what you need, without excess" to reading time.
